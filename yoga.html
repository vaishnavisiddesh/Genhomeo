YOGAM SHARANAM GACHAMI

Yoga is a series of movements and breathing exercises designed to strengthen the body and calm the mind. The word “yoga” comes from Sanskrit and means “to join,” or “to unite,” symbolizing the union of body and consciousness. Today, it's recognized not just as a form of exercise but also as a lifestyle that encompasses a philosophy of prioritizing balance.

IYENGAR YOGA

Precision and alignment are the hallmarks of iyengar yoga, which often incorporates props like blocks, straps, and bolsters to help maintain proper alignment. It can help with developing patience and mindfulness, and enhance mental clarity.
A precision-based practice that uses props like blocks and straps to perfect alignment. Iyengar yoga emphasizes holding poses longer to build strength and awareness. Ideal for individuals recovering from injuries or those wanting to deepen their practice. How to practice: Pay close attention to the alignment in each pose. Use props to aid in achieving the correct posture.

HATHA YOGA

Hatha yoga is a gentle form of yoga that can be a good place for beginners to start. It combines static poses with breathing to help promote relaxation and stress relief.
The foundation of many yoga styles, Hatha focuses on basic postures and breath control. It’s a great starting point for beginners, as it moves at a slow, deliberate pace, emphasizing balance and relaxation. How to practice: Focus on mastering the basics of each pose and breathing technique.

KUNDALINI YOGA

Kundalini yoga is more spiritual, aiming to unlock the energy at the base of the spine. It involves chanting, meditation, and specific poses to help enhance self-awareness and inner peace.How to practice: Engage in the chanting and breathing exercises with an open mind and heart.


Known as the "yoga of awareness," Kundalini combines dynamic movements, breathwork, and meditation to awaken spiritual energy. It’s a deeply transformative practice that promotes mental clarity and emotional resilience.
BIKRAM YOGA

Performed in a hot room with a series of 26 poses, bikram yoga focuses on detoxification. It is challenging, so it can help build determination and mental resilience.
A challenging sequence of 26 poses practiced in a heated room (about 40°C). This intense style helps improve flexibility, detoxify the body through sweat, and build stamina. Be prepared for a rigorous experience! How to practice: Hydrate well and be prepared for intense heat. Focus on each pose and your endurance.

ASTHANGA YOGA

Ashtanga yoga is a rigorous style of yoga following a specific sequence of poses. It can help improve focus and discipline and reduce stress.
A structured and disciplined practice with six series of poses that progress in difficulty. Ashtanga focuses on breath, posture, and a set sequence, making it ideal for those who thrive on routine and self-discipline. How to practice: Learn the sequence and focus on smooth transitions and breath control.

RESTORATIVE YOGA

Restorative yoga is a relaxing style of yoga in which poses are held for longer periods, often involving props for support. It can help reduce anxiety and promote deep relaxation.
A deeply relaxing practice that uses props to support long-held poses. The goal is to release tension and encourage deep rest. Perfect for stress relief and recovery from physical or emotional strain. How to practice: Use props to comfortably hold poses. Focus on relaxation and letting go.

YIN YOGA

Yin yoga targets deep connective tissues, as it requires you to hold poses for extended periods of time. It can help cultivate patience and improve flexibility.
A meditative practice that targets deep connective tissues by holding poses for several minutes. It enhances flexibility, circulation, and joint mobility. Great for those seeking stillness and introspection. How to practice: Stay in each pose for several minutes, focusing on deep breathing and relaxation.

ANUSARA YOGA

This modern, spiritually oriented system has an emphasis on alignment. Anusara yoga can help boost self-esteem and inner positivity.This modern, spiritually oriented system has an emphasis on alignment. Anusara yoga can help boost self-esteem and inner positivity.


A heart-centered practice that blends alignment with uplifting philosophy. It encourages self-expression and spiritual connection, making it a joyful and empowering experience.
PARENTAL YOGA

Prenatal yoga is tailored for pregnant women, focusing on poses that increase flexibility and strength. It can help reduce pregnancy-related stress and anxiety, and prepare the body for childbirth.
Designed for expecting mothers, prenatal yoga focuses on gentle poses, breathwork, and relaxation techniques to support pregnancy and childbirth. It helps reduce discomfort and promotes emotional well-being. How to practice: Concentrate on gentle poses and breathing exercises that are safe for pregnancy. Modify poses as needed for comfort and support.

VINYASA YOGA

Known for its fluid and dynamic movements, with each pose smoothly transitioning to the next, vinyasa yoga is like a dance. Synchronizing breath with movement, it can help enhance body awareness, reduce stress, and improve focus and concentration.
A dynamic practice that links breath with movement, Vinyasa focuses on fluid transitions between poses. It builds strength, flexibility, and endurance while promoting mindfulness. Great for those who enjoy creative sequences and an active flow. How to practice: Focus on the flow of movement and breath to find rhythm in the movements.
